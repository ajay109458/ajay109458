## Who I Am

Staff Software Engineer at Google, previously at Microsoft and Disney+ Hotstar. My career has been centered on one problem: making large-scale infrastructure reliable, understandable, and boring in the best possible way.

I've built sovereign cloud environments for multiple nations, operated Kubernetes orchestration at 5,000-cluster scale, and run DNS services carrying billions of queries per day. The work that interests me most is where complexity has real consequences — where a bad design decision doesn't just slow you down, it takes down production.

What I optimize for:

- Reliability before impressiveness
- Systems that scale without becoming fragile
- Reducing operational toil without sacrificing observability
- Making complexity measurable and automatable

> I trust systems that are calm at 3 a.m. more than systems that are exciting at 3 p.m.

## Featured Work

These are highlights from my career. Most of this work was internal; where I have public work, it's linked directly.

### Cosmic — Cloud Infrastructure Platform (Microsoft Azure)

Designed and operated the platform automating provisioning, deployment, and management across **5,000+ AKS clusters** and **6.7M+ cores** at peak scale. The system handled the full lifecycle: bootstrap, scheduling, upgrade orchestration, and failure recovery — with zero-touch operations as the target state.

`Azure` `Kubernetes` `Go` `Container Orchestration` `Infrastructure Automation`

---

### Azure Sovereign Clouds — France, Germany, Singapore (Microsoft)

Built sovereign cloud infrastructure for multiple nations from the ground up. Each environment required isolated networking, sovereign DNS, and compliance with strict data-residency regulations — all while maintaining operational parity with global Azure.

`Azure` `Sovereign Cloud` `Networking` `DNS` `Compliance Infrastructure`

---

### DNS at Sovereign Scale (Microsoft)

Designed and operated mission-critical DNS services for sovereign cloud regions. The system handled billions of queries per day with five-nines availability requirements, strict latency SLAs, and no tolerance for data exfiltration across trust boundaries.

`Azure DNS` `Distributed Systems` `Reliability Engineering` `Observability`
